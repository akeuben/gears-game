How Much Time Does School Really Take?

A general rule of thumb is to plan for 2–3 hours of study for every 1 hour of class. That means just one course can take 5–9 hours per week. Science courses with labs require even more time, including extra hours for lab work and preparation.

Let's look at an example

Anna's Weekly Study Plan

Anna is taking 3 science courses and 2 electives during the fall term. Here's how she's planning her time:

  • For science courses, she's dedicating 3 hours of study ofr each hour of lecture because the content is difficult
  • Two of her science courses include labs, which add more time.
  • For her electives (social sciences), she plans 2 hours of study per lecture hour.

Here's what her weekly schedule looks like

Course Class Time (hrs) Lab Time (hrs) Study Time (hrs) Total per Week
Biology 3 3 9 15
Chemistry 3 3 9 15
Genetics 3 - 9 12
English 3 - 6 9
History 3 - 6 9
TOTAL 15 6 39 60

If Anna follows this plan, she'll spend 60 hours per week on school. She'll also need to make time for meals, chores, social activities, and possibly a part-time job.
