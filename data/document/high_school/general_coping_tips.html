Entering postsecondary education is a time of significant transition and personal growth. For many students, it marks the beginning of independent living, increased academic expectations, and new social environments. While these experiences can be exciting, they also bring about considerable stress and pressure.


Coping helps us reduce stress. Coping strategies can be good or bad with some more effective than others. For example, consider responding to a stressful situation by emotional eating versus seeking out support from friends.

Learning and applying effective coping strategies is important for success. It helps us adapt to challenges, and maintain emotional balance. This blog provides some suggestions for you as you start to build a diverse toolkit of coping strategies to thrive.

1. Master Time Management
Use planners, to-do lists, or digital apps to organize tasks. Break large assignments into smaller steps and set realistic deadlines.
2. Create a Routine
Having consistent sleep, study, and meal times helps reduce stress and build structure into your day.
3. Stay Active
Physical activity—even a short walk—can boost your mood, improve focus, and reduce anxiety.
4. Set Boundaries with Screens
Limit time on social media or gaming. Too much screen time can increase stress and disrupt sleep.
5. Use Academic Supports
Struggling with coursework? Reach out to your school’s tutoring center, writing help, or talk to your profs. You don’t have to do it alone.
6. Shift Your Self-Talk
Notice your inner critic. Try to reframe negative thoughts with kind, realistic ones. You’re doing your best—and that’s enough.
7. Connect with Others
Reach out to friends, family, or classmates. A quick chat or study session can make a big difference.
8. Ask for Help When You Need It
If things feel overwhelming, talk to a counselor or check in with your campus health services. Support is there for you.


Learning to cope doesn’t eliminate challenges, but it empowers you to respond to them constructively. Take time to try out these strategies and to come up with some of your own.
