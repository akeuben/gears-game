When a psychoeducational assessment takes place, psychologists write a report that includes information about how they conducted the assessment, their main findings and conclusions, and their recommendations for supporting your learning. Copies of reports are given to clients after going over the results of the assessment.

If you do not know where your report is, ask your parents/caregiver where it might be or how you might get a copy.


People to Ask:

  • Your school. They employ psychologists to do this kind of work and/or sometimes parents/caregivers give them a copy for their file.
  • The psychologist in a private practice.
  • The physician (this might be a short report or a doctor's note).

If you cannot find it, you can ask the organization or person who did the assessment for a copy. Records like these must be stored for 10 years or longer in case you need a copy.


If the report cannot be found, schools will have an Individual Program Plan (or Individual Education Plan or Learning Support Plan) and it will often include information from the report. You can ask for a copy of that document from your school.


Note:

Some parents/caregivers may not want their kids to see the report. There are many different reasons for this, but one reason might be that they have an issue with wording in the report. If this is the case, it is best to respect your parent and not look at the report. Your Individual Education Plan is a good alternative.
