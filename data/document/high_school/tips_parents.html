• Most parents/caregivers are open with their kids about their diagnosis. If your parents are okay with being asked, go for it!
  • If you are unsure how to ask, here is some suggested wording:

    "I'm planning to apply to postsecondary and I'm learning about how to prepare for it with this program called 'GEARS'. GEARS suggests that I learn more about my diagnosis and how it affects my learning. I'd like to know more about what my diagnosis is. Can you tell me what you know about my diagnosis?"

  • It might be that the person you live with now wasn't part of the assessment process and does not know much about your diagnosis. If that is the case, you can talk to a teacher or guidance counsellor.
