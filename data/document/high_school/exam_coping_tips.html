Exams can be stressful—but with the right strategies, you can get through them with more confidence and less chaos.

Whether you are facing your first round of university finals or you’re a seasoned student, here are some ways to manage exam pressure while taking care of your mental and physical health.

1. Create a Study Schedule
Don’t just wing it. Creating a clear, realistic study schedule helps you feel in control. Break your subjects into chunks, focus on what matters most, and schedule breaks so your brain has time to recharge.
2. Sleep is Study Fuel
Think sleep doesn’t matter? Think again. Sleep helps your brain process and store information. Aim for 7–9 hours each night, especially before a big exam. Ditch the all-nighter—it hurts more than it helps.
3. Eat (and Drink) Like You Care
Fuel your body with regular, balanced meals. Keep healthy snacks nearby and don’t forget to hydrate. Yes, coffee is fine—but water is better for long-term energy and focus.
4. Calm Your Nerves with Relaxation
Even just 5 minutes of deep breathing, meditation, or stretching can reset your stress levels. Try a mindfulness app or take a few deep breaths before you hit the books again.
5. Eliminate Distractions
Find a quiet space, put your phone on “Do Not Disturb,” and use a website blocker if needed. Fewer distractions = more done in less time",
6. Study Smarter, Not Longer
Instead of re-reading your notes endlessly, try active study techniques:
- Use flashcards
- Teach the material to someone else
- Do past exams or practice questions
These methods help your brain engage and retain better.
7. Stay Connected
You’re not alone—reach out to classmates, friends, or study groups. Talking through tricky topics (or just venting) can ease stress and give you fresh perspective.
8. Take Breaks That Actually Work
Short breaks every hour boost productivity. Step away from the screen, move your body, or get some air. A walk around the block can do wonders.
9. Be Kind to Yourself
You don’t need to ace everything. Give yourself credit for showing up and doing your best. Practice self-compassion—it’s a better motivator than criticism.
10. Know When to Reach Out
If stress feels like too much, you’re not weak—you’re human. Contact your school’s mental health services, peer support, or a counselor. Asking for help is a strength.


Exams are a challenge, but they don’t have to break you. With a few intentional choices, you can reduce stress and show up as your best self. Be organized, take care of your body and mind, and remember: you’ve got this.
