How do I get supports for my learning at postsecondary?

Institutions are responsible for ensuring students with disabilities have equal access to quality education. It is a human right. Postsecondary students are assured equal access to education through accommodations and assistive technology.

Most institutions have accessibility offices that provide supports for students but the office names can vary. For example, they might be called “access and inclusion offices” or “services for students with disabilities.” Staff at accessibility services offices are trained to identify assistive technology that help you learn the best.

The information you provide is confidential and is not shared with instructors or other staff.

Why should I register with accessibility services?

If you are not registered, instructors do not have to provide accommodations. Unfortunately, if you wait too long to get connected with services you could experience a delay getting an appointment to see someone and then have to wait a while to get accommodations. Depending on the waitlist, but the time everything is set up the first term might be over!

Staff at accessibility services help to keep instructors accountable for providing accommodations.

And the best thing – the service is free.

How do I make an appointment?

Check the website of the school you plan to attend. Most schools have an online booking system.

When should I make an appointment?

It can take time to organize academic accommodation and funding for disability-related services. Begin the registration process several months before you start. Make an appointment immediately after receiving your acceptance.

What do I need to bring to my first appointment?

Check the website of the office, but most will require documentation of your disability.

Your institution may also ask you to complete an intake form or other forms.
