How will you pay for postsecondary?

There are many sources for funding. It is important to identify how you can fun your education and if you will have enough funding to afford schooling.

Savings & Family Contributions

Some parents have saved money for their children to attend postsecondary or you may have saved money yourself. Registered Education Savings Plans (RESPs) are one way parents save money for a child’s education after high school.

https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/education/education-savings.html

On and Off-Campus Employment

Some students work part-time while they study to cover educational costs and living expenses. Working can reduce reliance on loans, but you should be cautious about the impact it might have on your academic performance. Research shows that for typical students, working 20 + hours/week has a negative impact on academic performance. This might be different for students with disabilities who need to spend more time studying and learning material.

Borrowing

Canada Student Loans are available to Canadian citizens and permanent residents who are studying full- or part-time and demonstrate financial need. You will apply through your home province or territory of residence. Only once you stop being a student, you will have to start repaying loans and interest will begin to add up. Make sure you have a plan for repayment once you have completed your education.

https://ambrose.edu/student-loans

Some banks offer Student Lines of Credit: A line of credit is a credit product that lets you borrow money repeatedly up to a pre-set limit. You can borrow money, pay it back and then borrow again, up to your credit limit. However, you will have to pay interest on any money you borrow while you are studying. Know your rights before you get a line of credit.

https://www.canada.ca/en/financial-consumer-agency/services/loans/student-lines-credit.html

You might be tempted to use credit cards to cover expenses. Credit cards are like a line of credit but with much greater interest rates and it is an expensive way to borrow money. Only use your credit card to cover expenses if you are prepared to pay them off what you have borrowed the end of each month.

https://www.canada.ca/en/financial-consumer-agency/services/credit-cards/credit-card-work.html

For other information about loans see: https://www.csnpe-nslsc.canada.ca/en/managing-your-money/understanding-your-loan

Grants

Disability Grants:

  • Students with disabilities who have a Canada Student Loan may be eligible for a Canada Student Grants for Students with Disabilities and the Canada Student Grant for Services and Equipment.
  • The Canada Student Grant is available once per loan year, is reassessed each year, and is part of your loan. It does not have to be repaid.
  • The Grant for Services and Equipment is to cover costs associated with your disability. You must have documentation that your disability restricts your ability to perform daily activities necessary to pursue studies at the postsecondary level.
  • Ask about grants when you meet with an advisor at accessibility services.

Indigenous Grants:

  • Bands provide grants to Indigenous students completing full-time studies. Each band has its own process for applying and awarding grants. See other opportunities here: https://www.alberta.ca/resources-for-indigenous-students

Scholarships and Awards

A variety of scholarships and bursaries are available to students, both through their postsecondary and from external sources. For example: https://ambrose.edu/financial-aid/sources-financial-assistance

Plan to apply for scholarships and awards when you apply to the school or check out the deadline and make note of the date.

See also, the Federal Government’s information page about paying for your education: https://www.csnpe-nslsc.canada.ca/en/managing-your-money/paying-for-your-education
